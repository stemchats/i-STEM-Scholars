i-STEM Scholars Summer Research Program

The annual i-STEM Scholars Summer Research Program, which connects high school students with university labs to contribute to a research project for about 10 weeks. Students are also provided with academic and professional development resources, as well as personalized mentorship. Our Summer Research Program is application-based, with a focus on students who are FGLI, URM, LGBTQ+, and women, and students spend about 3 months with accredited research mentors.

Summer Research Program - 2020 Cohort

For our first-ever Summer Research Program cohort in 2020, the i-STEM Scholars team partnered with the Stockton Lab at the Georgia Institute of Technology. The i-STEM Scholars SRP hosted 10 students, who each conducted research, participated in academic and professional development, and received personal mentorship. The research topic was chemical analyses of meteorites for potential signs of life.

Dr. Amanda Stockton

Principal Investigator

I am so impressed with the diverse backgrounds and diverse interests of the students. The students contributed so much to our research, and they all learned things that are not just within our little pocket of research, but are also applicable the other fields [of STEM] the students are interested in. Some of these concepts such as enantiomers, spectrometry, and spectroscopy are something that chemistry majors learn in the second, third, and fourth year of college. The students also learned a lot about how to read scientific literature, which most students don't even get comprehensive discussion until a graduate/PhD program.
